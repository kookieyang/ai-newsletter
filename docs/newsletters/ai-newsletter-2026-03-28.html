ILO-World Bank Study: GenAI Will Disrupt Developing World Faster Than It Helps
A joint ILO–World Bank study released March 27, covering 135 countries and two-thirds of global employment, warns that developing economies may experience AI-driven job disruption faster than productivity gains. Workers in automatable jobs in lower-income countries are often already online, making them vulnerable to swift displacement — particularly women and young people in higher-quality service roles. Meanwhile, workers who could benefit from AI productivity boosts often lack internet access entirely. The study urges investment in digital connectivity, skills development, and social protection systems.

Vietnam's AI Law Takes Effect — First Comprehensive Standalone AI Regime in Southeast Asia
Vietnam became the first Southeast Asian nation with a comprehensive standalone AI regime when its Law on Artificial Intelligence took effect on March 1, 2026. Legacy high-risk systems in health, education, and finance have up to 18 months to comply.
